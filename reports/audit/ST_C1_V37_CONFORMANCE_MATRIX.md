## Two required examples (task spec)

- **Example 1 — valid bearish candidate**: `tests/test_signal_v37_gates.py::test_example_1_valid_bearish_candidate_reaches_candidate_ready` — confirmed bearish HTF structure, selected dealing range, price in premium, fresh bearish H1 order-block POI causally linked to a qualifying displacement, M5 buy-side (bear-dir) sweep, bearish close-confirmed CHoCH, next-bar-open entry, stop above the causal displacement origin, external sell-side liquidity target, reward well above the net 3R floor. Result: `CANDIDATE_READY`.
- **Example 2 — visually attractive but invalid bullish candidate**: `tests/test_signal_v37_gates.py::test_example_2_bullish_candidate_records_both_location_and_net_rr_evidence` — bullish M5 sweep+displacement genuinely present, but entry lands in HTF premium (should be discount), and a separate, clean numeric illustration (`test_g8_net_rr_formula_gross_clears_but_net_misses_threshold`) shows a 3.20R gross / 2.89R net case matching the task's 3.2R/2.85R scenario. Result: rejection with **both** `G4_WRONG_SIDE_OF_EQUILIBRIUM` (primary) and `G8_NET_RR_BELOW_THRESHOLD` (secondary) recorded on the same gate trace — required a pipeline change (see §4 of the traceability matrix) so G4 does not short-circuit before G8 evidence can be computed.

## What this matrix does NOT claim

Per this task's own instruction, passing these unit/integration tests is
evidence that the RULES are correctly, point-in-time, executable — it is
**not** evidence of net trading edge. See
`reports/audit/ST_C1_V37_FINAL_VALIDATION_DECISION.md` for the separate,
real-data ablation result and validation classification.
